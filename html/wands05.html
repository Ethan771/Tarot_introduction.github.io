Five of Wands

Symbolism: The Five of Wands typically depicts five individuals, each holding a wand, seemingly in conflict with each other. They might appear to be fighting, competing, or trying to work together but struggling. This imagery represents conflict, competition, and challenges.

Upright Meaning: When the Five of Wands appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies conflict, competition, and challenges.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Five of Wands can indicate resolution, avoiding conflict, or internal struggles.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Five of Wands often serves as a reflection of the challenges and conflicts one might face, whether with others or within oneself. It suggests a period of competition, disagreements, or obstacles that require effort to navigate. The card emphasizes the importance of understanding different perspectives and finding ways to collaborate or resolve disputes. When reversed, it indicates a move towards resolution, collaboration, or overcoming challenges.
