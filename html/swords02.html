Two of Swords

Symbolism: The Two of Swords typically depicts a woman sitting on a bench or stone seat, blindfolded, holding two swords crossed over her chest. Behind her, there might be a body of water with rocks or a distant land, symbolizing the emotional and mental challenges she faces. The blindfold and crossed swords represent a stalemate, indecision, and the need for clarity.

Upright Meaning: When the Two of Swords appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies indecision, stalemate, and blocked emotions.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Two of Swords can indicate clarity, making a decision, or releasing blocked emotions.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Two of Swords often highlights a period of indecision or feeling stuck in a situation where there's no clear path forward. It serves as a reminder to take a step back, seek clarity, and trust one's intuition when making decisions. The card emphasizes the importance of facing one's emotions and not avoiding the truth, even if it's uncomfortable. When reversed, it suggests that clarity is on the horizon, and a decision will soon be made.
