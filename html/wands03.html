Three of Wands

Symbolism: The Three of Wands typically depicts a figure standing on a hill or elevated ground, looking out over a vast landscape or sea, with three wands standing upright around him. Ships might be seen in the distance, suggesting trade or journeys. This imagery represents foresight, expansion, and awaiting results.

Upright Meaning: When the Three of Wands appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies looking ahead, expansion, and anticipation.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Three of Wands can indicate delays, lack of foresight, or missed opportunities.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Three of Wands often serves as a symbol of anticipation and expansion. It suggests that one's efforts are starting to bear fruit, and it's a time of looking forward to the results. The card emphasizes the importance of preparation and being open to growth and new horizons. When reversed, it serves as a reminder to plan adequately and be patient, ensuring that one is ready to seize opportunities when they arise.
