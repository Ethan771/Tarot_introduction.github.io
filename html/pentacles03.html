Three of Pentacles

Symbolism: The Three of Pentacles typically depicts a scene where an artisan or craftsman is at work, often sculpting or building something in a cathedral or a similar setting. He is usually shown interacting with two other figures, which can represent clients, employers, or colleagues. The imagery of the card emphasizes collaboration, craftsmanship, and the initial stages of a project coming to fruition.

Upright Meaning: When the Three of Pentacles appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies collaboration, skill, and the value of teamwork.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Three of Pentacles can indicate lack of teamwork, disregard for skills, or a project facing obstacles.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Three of Pentacles often serves as a reminder of the value of collaboration, skill, and planning. It suggests that by working together with others and valuing each person's contributions, you can achieve great things. It's also a card that emphasizes the importance of recognizing and utilizing one's skills and talents.
