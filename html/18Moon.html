The Moon (Card XVIII)

Symbolism: The Moon card often features a large, glowing moon in the sky, flanked by two towers or pillars and a body of water below. A crayfish or lobster is usually seen emerging from the water, and a path leads away into the distance. Wolves or dogs may howl at the moon, representing the call of the wild or the realm of the unconscious. The card is rich in symbolism related to intuition, dreams, and the subconscious mind.

Upright Meaning: When the Moon card appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies mystery, intuition, and the unconscious mind. The card encourages you to pay attention to your dreams, instincts, and emotional reactions. It's a card of illusion and confusion, often appearing when things are not as they seem or when you're navigating through emotional or psychological fog.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Moon card can indicate clarity, truth, or the dispelling of illusions. It suggests that things are becoming clearer or that you're gaining a better understanding of your emotional or psychological state.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Moon card serves as a reminder to trust your intuition and to be cautious in situations where all the facts are not clear. Whether you're dealing with emotional confusion, mysterious circumstances, or complex psychological issues, this card encourages you to delve into your subconscious mind for answers. It's a card that often appears when you're navigating through emotional waters or dealing with situations that are not as they seem.
