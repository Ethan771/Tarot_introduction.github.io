Six of cups

Symbolism: The Six of Cups typically depicts two children in a garden or courtyard, with one child handing a cup filled with flowers to the other. Around them, there are usually five other cups, also filled with flowers. The imagery evokes feelings of nostalgia, innocence, and simpler times. The card's atmosphere is one of warmth, sharing, and pleasant memories.

Upright Meaning: When the Six of Cups appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies nostalgia, pleasant memories, and a return to innocence. It can represent a period of reflection on past joys or the rekindling of old relationships.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Six of Cups can indicate being stuck in the past, naivety, or an inability to move forward.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Six of Cups often serves as a reminder to cherish pleasant memories and to reconnect with your inner child. Whether you're reminiscing about the past, rekindling an old relationship, or simply enjoying a moment of innocence and simplicity, this card encourages you to embrace the warmth and joy of nostalgia.
