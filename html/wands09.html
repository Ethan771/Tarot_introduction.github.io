Nine of Wands

Symbolism: The Nine of Wands typically depicts a figure standing with a wand in hand, looking weary but determined. Behind him, there are eight other wands standing upright, forming a sort of barrier or defensive line. The figure might appear bruised or bandaged, suggesting past battles or challenges. This imagery represents resilience, persistence, and preparedness.

Upright Meaning: When the Nine of Wands appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies resilience, determination, and caution.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Nine of Wands can indicate paranoia, unnecessary defenses, or giving up.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Nine of Wands often serves as a symbol of resilience and determination. It suggests that one has faced many challenges but remains standing, ready to face whatever comes next. The card emphasizes the importance of learning from past experiences and being prepared, but also serves as a reminder not to be overly defensive or wary. When reversed, it's a nudge to assess whether one's defenses are necessary and to ensure that one doesn't give up too easily.
