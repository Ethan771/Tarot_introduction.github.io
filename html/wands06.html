Six of Wands

Symbolism: The Six of Wands typically depicts a figure on horseback, holding a wand adorned with a laurel wreath, symbolizing victory. The figure is often shown riding through a crowd of admirers or supporters, suggesting a triumphant return or parade. This imagery represents success, recognition, and public acclaim.

Upright Meaning: When the Six of Wands appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies success, recognition, and achievement.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Six of Wands can indicate a lack of recognition, falling short, or false success.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Six of Wands often serves as a symbol of success and recognition. It suggests that one's efforts are being acknowledged and celebrated, and it's a time of pride and accomplishment. The card emphasizes the joy of achievement and the importance of recognizing and celebrating successes, both big and small. When reversed, it serves as a reminder to stay humble, ensure that success is genuine, and understand that recognition may sometimes be delayed.
