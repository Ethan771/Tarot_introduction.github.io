Three of Swords

Symbolism: The Three of Swords typically depicts a heart pierced by three swords against a backdrop of rain or storm clouds. This stark and poignant imagery represents heartbreak, sorrow, and emotional pain.

Upright Meaning: When the Three of Swords appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies heartbreak, pain, and separation.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Three of Swords can indicate healing, reconciliation, or the easing of pain.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Three of Swords often serves as a stark reminder of the emotional challenges and pains that are a part of the human experience. It suggests that one might be going through a period of heartbreak, grief, or emotional turmoil. The card emphasizes the importance of acknowledging and processing these emotions, rather than suppressing or avoiding them. When reversed, it offers a glimmer of hope, indicating that healing and reconciliation are possible, and that the intensity of pain will eventually subside.
