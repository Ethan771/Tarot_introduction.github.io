The Hermit (Card IX)

Symbolism: The Hermit is usually depicted as an old, wise man standing alone on a mountain, holding a lantern that illuminates his path. The lantern often contains a six-pointed star, symbolizing wisdom and the divine. He may also hold a staff, representing power and authority, and is often cloaked, symbolizing introspection and withdrawal from the external world.

Upright Meaning: When the Hermit appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies solitude, introspection, and wisdom. The card encourages you to take some time away from the hustle and bustle of everyday life to reflect on your inner world. It's a card of seeking and enlightenment, urging you to look for the answers within.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Hermit can indicate isolation, loneliness, or excessive introspection. It suggests that you may be withdrawing too much from the world or becoming too focused on your inner thoughts, leading to a sense of isolation or disconnection.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Hermit serves as a reminder to take some time for introspection and self-reflection. Whether you're facing a difficult decision, seeking spiritual enlightenment, or simply feeling overwhelmed by the demands of daily life, this card encourages you to retreat inward and seek the wisdom that resides within you. It's a card that often appears when you're at a crossroads and need to make a decision based on deep, inner understanding.
