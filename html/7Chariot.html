The Chariot (Card VII)

Symbolism: The Chariot is often depicted as a warrior or a hero riding a chariot, usually pulled by two sphinxes or horses of contrasting colors, symbolizing duality and opposing forces. The charioteer holds a wand or a scepter, representing willpower, and wears armor, indicating protection and readiness for battle. Above his head, there may be a canopy of stars, signifying divine protection or guidance, and he often bears symbols of victory and conquest.

Upright Meaning: When the Chariot appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies willpower, determination, and victory. The card encourages you to harness your inner strength and to channel your energies toward achieving your goals. It's a card of triumph, often appearing when you're highly focused and have a clear sense of purpose.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Chariot can indicate a lack of control, direction, or willpower. It suggests that you may be letting external circumstances dictate your actions or that you're struggling with internal conflicts that are preventing you from moving forward.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Chariot serves as a powerful reminder that you have the strength and willpower to overcome challenges and achieve your goals. Whether you're facing a difficult situation at work, in a relationship, or in any other aspect of your life, this card encourages you to take control and direct your energies in a focused manner. It's a card that often appears when you're on the brink of a significant achievement or breakthrough.
