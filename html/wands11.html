Page of Wands

Symbolism: The Page of Wands typically depicts a young figure holding a staff or wand, standing in a desert-like landscape. The figure often appears enthusiastic, curious, and ready for adventure. This imagery represents exploration, enthusiasm, and new beginnings.

Upright Meaning: When the Page of Wands appears in a reading in its upright position, it often signifies enthusiasm, exploration, and discovery.

Reversed Meaning: In its reversed position, the Page of Wands can indicate lack of direction, procrastination, or unfulfilled potential.

In a Reading: In a Tarot reading, the Page of Wands often serves as a symbol of enthusiasm and exploration. It suggests that one is on the brink of a new adventure or discovery, filled with excitement and curiosity. The card emphasizes the importance of embracing new beginnings and being open to inspiration. When reversed, it's a reminder to ensure that enthusiasm is channeled effectively and that one doesn't rush into things without proper consideration.
